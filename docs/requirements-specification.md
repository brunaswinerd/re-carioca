# Requirements Specification  
## Re-Carioca Project  
Academic Extension Project – Web Front-End

---

## 1. Introduction

This document describes the requirements specification for the **Re-Carioca Project**, developed as part of the Web Front-End Extension Course at Centro Universitário UniCarioca.

The document supports the structured organization and execution of the project, serving as a foundation for both technical and functional decisions.

---

## 2. Thematic Axis

The project was inspired by practical experience in thrift stores, highlighting the importance of the circular economy in addressing contemporary consumption challenges.

The proposal goes beyond commercial aspects and promotes:

- Environmental sustainability  
- Social responsibility  
- Democratization of access to goods  
- Solidarity and inclusion  

---

## 3. Project Proposal

Development of a digital platform for a fictional charitable bazaar aimed at collecting and redistributing donations to low-income residents of Tijuca (Rio de Janeiro) and surrounding neighborhoods.

The project integrates:

- Business rules structuring  
- Practical front-end development  
- Database modeling  
- Backend integration planning (in development)  

---

## 4. Identified Problem

- Difficulty accessing good-condition goods  
- Lack of a structured redistribution channel  
- Waste of reusable resources  
- Limited circular economy practices in the region  

The system aims to safely and inclusively connect donors, beneficiaries, and volunteers.

---

## 5. Requirements Gathering Methodology

The requirements were identified through:

- Google Forms survey to collect preferences  
- Analysis of similar platforms (Exército de Salvação, Doações.gov.br, Doare.org)  
- Observation of charitable thrift stores in Tijuca  

---

## 6. Target Audience

### Donors
Individuals residing in Rio de Janeiro or other locations.

### Beneficiaries
Low-income residents of:

- Tijuca  
- Maracanã  
- Vila Isabel  
- Andaraí  
- Grajaú  
- Rio Comprido  

**Income Criteria:**

- Up to 1 minimum wage per capita (families)  
- Up to 2 minimum wages (individual)  

### Volunteers
Responsible for:

- Internal organization  
- Donation collection  
- Product delivery  

---

## 7. Accepted Product Categories

### Clothing
- Women’s, men’s, and children’s clothing  
- Winter items (coats, hats, scarves)  
- Underwear, swimwear, and socks (new condition only)

### Accessories
- Bags  
- Backpacks  
- Shoes (good condition only)

### Books
- Preference for updated educational and technical materials  
- No +18 content

### Electronics
- Small and large home appliances  
- Electronic devices (phones, tablets, notebooks, etc.)

### Furniture
- Small and large household furniture  
- Mattresses only new or in excellent condition  

---

## 8. Acquisition Limits

Defined to ensure fair distribution.

### Clothing
Up to 3 items every 3 months  

### Accessories
1 item every 3 months  

### Shoes
1 pair every 3 months  

### Books
1 item every 3 months  

### Electronics
- Small appliances: 12 months  
- Large appliances: 18 months  
- Digital devices: 6 to 18 months depending on type  

### Furniture
- Small furniture: 12 months  
- Large furniture: 18 months  

---

## 9. Tie-Breaking Criteria

- Lower household income  
- Justification provided  
- Priority for public school students  

---

## 10. Donor Rules

- Items must be clean and functional  
- Form completion required  
- Photos and description required  
- Responsibility for the accuracy of information  

---

## 11. Beneficiary Rules

- Residence in Tijuca or surrounding neighborhoods  
- Form completion required  
- Proof of income required  
- Accuracy of provided information  

---

## 12. Collection and Delivery

### Donations
- In-person delivery  
- Volunteer pickup (scheduled)  
- Possible symbolic fee for large volumes  

### Withdrawal
- In-person pickup  
- Volunteer delivery (fuel fee if necessary)  
- 7-day deadline  

---

## 13. System and Technologies

### Platform Features
- Responsive web platform  
- Accessibility (Semantic HTML + WAI-ARIA)  
- PWA (offline functionality)  
- Form validation  

### Technologies Used
- HTML5  
- Tailwind CSS  
- JavaScript (ES6+)  
- Vue 3  
- PWA  
- Node.js  
- VS Code
